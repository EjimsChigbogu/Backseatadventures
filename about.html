ABOUT BACKSEATADVENTURES

Backseat Adventures Project is our response to God’s call for us to document the untold stories of great missionaries and pioneers from Africa, preserving these stories for the coming generations to hear, read and watch. We gain a deeper understanding of God by studying His dealings and relationships with people. The lives and stories of these pioneers have a lot to reveal to coming generations about God, His heart for all nations, how He calls men, walks with them and how He is faithful to all his promises. These stories of extreme sacrifices made by men and women, of obedience, surrender, consecration and daily dependence on God will inspire another generation to deeper commitments to walking with God. There are also stories of mistakes and of second chances, revealing the true nature of God’s Grace and mercy. Ultimately, this project is not about the stories of men and women, it is about telling the story of God through the lives of these heroes of faith, many of whom don’t consider themselves heroes, but whose lives of simplicity and obedience has pushed the frontiers of God’s Kingdom and brought salvation to families, villages, cities and nations.

How we are doing this

We have a team of volunteers who will visit the missionaries at their locations. Typical visits may last days or weeks allowing us to familiarize with the missionary and be able to capture most parts of their stories. The sessions would include official video recording sessions, interview sessions, informal fact gathering and where need be, documentary style recordings or event re-creation.
Recordings would later be edited and produced, while also being transcribed into books for written documentation.
